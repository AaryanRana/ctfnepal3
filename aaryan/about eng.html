About Engage Nepal With Science


Information

Engage Nepal with Science is a project created by Alba Abad, a researcher at the Welcome Centre for Cell Biology. It's a partnership between the Research Institute for Bioscience and Biotechnology (RIBB) in Nepal and the Wellcome Centre for Cell Biology at the University of Edinburgh in the UK. Engage Nepal with Science aims to spread the culture of engaging with science and the scientific research carried out at Nepalese research centres to empower, inspire and build confidence in STEM (Science-Technology-Engineering-Mathematics).The project started in 2018, with the team working from different locations to plan and connect with teachers and researchers. They first adapted the project to schools' needs through consultations and built strong relationships with teachers. In March 2019, they met in person and began working with Nepalese communities.

Aims of Engage Nepal With Science

  • -Spread the culture of science
  • -Empower, inspire, and build confidence in STEM
  • -Promote critical and innovative thinking
  • -Create a fairer, more inclusive society
  • -Foster positive social change
  • -Offer development workshops to boost engagement and science communication skills
  • -Inspire with visits to research centers like RIBB
  • -Developing a culture of engaging Nepalese communities with science to inspire
  • -Provide schools with resources and different types of practical science workshops to complement the student's curriculum
